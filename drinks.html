Pina Colada

History and Origin

The Pina Colada, a tropical delight, traces its origins to Puerto Rico. It is believed to have been created in the 1950s or 1960s, with its precise inventor still debated. One story attributes its creation to a bartender named Ramón "Monchito" Marrero, who concocted the cocktail at the Caribe Hilton Hotel in San Juan. This creamy and sweet cocktail combines white rum, coconut cream, and pineapple juice, often garnished with a pineapple slice and maraschino cherry. The Pina Colada has become synonymous with leisurely vacations and beachside relaxation, making it an enduring symbol of Puerto Rican cocktail culture.

Ingredients

  • 50ml of Coconut washed Rum
  • 50ml of Coconut Milk
  • 15ml of Pinapple Syrup
  • 15ml of Coconut Syrup
  • 50ml of Pinapple Juice
  • Dehydrated Pinapple
  • Cubed Ice
  • Crushed Ice

Recipe

  1. Get your shaker, and add all the liquid ingredients
  2. Add Ice until you fill 3/4 of the Shaker
  3. Shake hard for 10 to 15 seconds
  4. Strain to a Glass filled with crushed ice
  5. Garnish with the Dehydrated Pinapple

Rum Old Fashioned

History and Origin

The Rum Old Fashioned is a variation of the classic Old Fashioned cocktail, and its history is closely linked to the broader history of the Old Fashioned itself. The Old Fashioned, considered one of the oldest known cocktails, dates back to the early 19th century when it was a simple mix of spirits, sugar, water, and bitters. The Rum Old Fashioned substitutes the traditional whiskey with rum, adding a distinct sweetness and complexity to the drink. While the exact origin of the Rum Old Fashioned may not be documented, it represents the evolution of cocktail culture and the enduring appeal of timeless recipes adapted to regional tastes and available ingredients, especially in Caribbean and tropical settings where rum is abundant.

Ingredients

  • 50ml of Age Rum such has El Dourado 12y
  • 2-3 dashes Angustura Bitters
  • 15ml od Demenara Sugar Syrup
  • 1 Orange Zest
  • Cubed Ice
  • 1 large Cubed Ice

Recipe

  1. Add all the liquid ingredientsto a Mixing Glass
  2. Fill the Mixing glass with Ice and Stir for 15-20 seconds
  3. Taste, the key for a good old Fashioned is Balance
  4. Serve in a rocks glass with a Large Ice Cube
  5. Express the Oils from the Orange Zest on to the glass
