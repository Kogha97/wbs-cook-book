Lentil Soup

History and Origin

Lentil soup boasts a rich history that spans millennia. Originating in the Middle East and North Africa, lentils have been cultivated and consumed since ancient times, with evidence of their presence dating back over 10,000 years. These protein-packed legumes were a staple in the diets of various ancient civilizations, including the Egyptians and Mesopotamians. As trade routes expanded, lentils spread across Europe and Asia, becoming a dietary cornerstone for diverse cultures

Ingredients

  • 1 Cup of red Lentils
  • 1 Medium size Onion
  • 1 Tablespoon of Flour
  • 2 Tablespoons of Oil
  • 6 Cups of hot Water ~1200ml
  • 1 Teaspoon of Salt
  • 1 Teaspoon of Turmeric

Recipe

  1. Heat oil in a pot, sauté chopped onions until slightly pink.
  2. Add flour and continue cooking on low heat.
  3. Rinse the lentils.
  4. Pour in water, mix, and cover. Bring to a boil, then simmer until lentils are soft.
  5. Blend the soup until smooth, or strain if you don't have a blender.
  6. Season with black pepper, salt, and optional cumin. Cook for 5 more minutes and remove from heat.
  7. If it's too thick, add some water and simmer briefly.
  8. In a small pan, melt two tablespoons of butter, add a teaspoon of red pepper powder, and remove from heat.
  9. Serve the lentil soup in bowls, drizzle with the red pepper sauce, and garnish with a lemon slice.

Garlic prawns, Camarão ao Alhinho

History and Origin

Portuguese garlic prawns, also known as "Camarões à Guilho," have a flavorful history that reflects Portugal's maritime heritage. This delectable dish originates from the coastal regions of Portugal, where fresh seafood has always played a central role in the cuisine. The key ingredients, prawns and garlic, were readily available to Portuguese fishermen, making it a convenient and delicious choice. Over time, the recipe evolved, incorporating aromatic herbs and spices like parsley and paprika to enhance the dish's complexity. Portuguese garlic prawns showcase the fusion of simple, high-quality ingredients and a passion for bold flavors that define Portuguese gastronomy, making it a beloved seafood classic both in Portugal and internationally.

Ingredients

  • 450g of Prawns
  • 6 Gloves of Garlic
  • 60ml of Olive Oil
  • 60ml of White Wine
  • 30g of salted Butter
  • Salt and Pepper to taste
  • Lemon wedges for garnish

Recipe

  1. Heat oil in a frying pan, sauté the Prawns.
  2. Add the Garlic either chopped or mashed
  3. Add Pepper and Salt.
  4. When the Prawns are almost cooked add Butter and White wine
  5. Reduce and sauce until prefered thickness
